FAQ

What is this?

Getting Unstuck is a free curriculum to inspire creativity with Scratch. The curriculum is composed of 10 modules, and each module supports students in creating a Scratch project focused on a particular prompt. Each module is organized around the creative computing design studio framework, which emphasizes learning through exploring, creating, sharing, and reflecting. Each module includes 18 activities, and while we expect from classroom testing that each module takes approximately 5 45-minute class sessions, project length can vary, depending on students' interests and classroom needs.

We chose the name Getting Unstuck to acknowledge the inherent challenges of creative work. As students pursue their creative dreams with code, there will likely be moments when they get stuck! We hope that the Getting Unstuck curriculum will support you and your students in navigating these moments and creating projects that are personally and socially meaningful.

Who is this for?

We designed this curriculum for teachers working with students in 4th, 5th, and/or 6th grade who have had some prior experience with Scratch, but the curriculum can be adapted for any grade level. This curriculum is designed for PK–12 teachers who are passionate or curious about supporting creative work with Scratch (a graphical programming language and online community being used by millions of young people around the world) in the classroom. Supporting students in pursuing creative work, with all of the twists and turns that can entail, can be tricky. As a teacher, in addition to having an adventurous spirit and genuine curiosity, it can help to have greater familiarity and fluency with Scratch.

What if I don't know anything about Scratch?

No problem! We recommend starting with our Creative Computing Curriculum for introductory programming experiences with Scratch and/or participating in a Scratch Educator Meetup.
